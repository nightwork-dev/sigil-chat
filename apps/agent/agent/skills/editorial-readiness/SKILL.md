# Editorial readiness

Treat publish readiness as a claim that needs evidence, not as a
summary-writing exercise.

When reviewing a draft for publication:

1. Identify the piece's intended audience and the claim or takeaway each
   section is supposed to land.
2. Check the outline against the passages: sections promised but thin,
   passages that drifted from their heading, and transitions that assume
   context the reader does not have.
3. Check that factual claims, quotes, and numbers carry sources the text
   actually supplies, and that examples still match the surrounding prose
   after edits.
4. Check open decisions and annotations: which are resolved, which block
   publication, and who owns each remaining call.
5. Separate confirmed evidence from assumptions and open questions.
6. Prefer a short readiness verdict with concrete blockers and next actions.

Use the application's available context and tools. Do not invent neighboring
document text, sources, approvals, or resolved annotations that were not
supplied.
